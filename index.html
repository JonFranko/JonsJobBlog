The real cost of “culture fit”

November 12, 2025 • 4–6 min read

When values become marketing, the bill always lands on workers. In healthy teams, anonymous surveys feed performance improvement loops. In unhealthy ones, they become ammunition for managing people out. Over the last few years I’ve lived on both sides of that line.

Under one manager, hard conversations about burnout were met with care and concrete adjustments. Under another—after a physical collapse—my “plan” was boiled down to a wellness quip. Same company, different culture in practice.

This blog is where I’ll map the gap between stated values and lived experience—without naming individuals, focusing instead on systems, incentives, and practical fixes leaders can deploy tomorrow. If you’ve seen similar gaps, I’d love to hear from you.
